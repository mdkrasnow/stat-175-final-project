There are two possible types of projects for this class. The groups should be 4 people.

Track 1: Applied investigation
You will choose a publicly available dataset for example here ( https://ogb.stanford.edu/ or https://pytorch-geometric.readthedocs.io/en/latest/modules/datasets.html) or anywhere else. You will formulate a scientific question on these datasets and use statistics and ML to solve them.
Dataset: You must select a publicly available dataset 
Scientific Question: Formulate a non-trivial hypothesis
Technical Requirements: 
Note that it is not enough to simply apply an existing statistics/GNN model to a new dataset. We expect to see some form of novelty (e.g. developing a new GNN method for your specific problem, improving on existing methods in a non-trivial way) or comprehensive analyses (e.g. ablation Studies, comparison between multiple model architectures, validation ) for the project or adapt a method from a research paper.
Expect to justify design choices, validation methods ect..

Track 2: Theoretical & Exploratory Research
This track is for students who wish to delve into the modern research frontiers of network science. T
Scope: You will select a modern research area ( I provide below examples of these later) and read 2–3 foundational papers (we can help with this task).
The " New Something" (Technical Contribution): You are required to produce one of the following:
A Small Theoretical Extension: Take a theorem from your chosen papers and relax an assumption. For example, if a convergence rate is proved for dense graphs, derive how the bound changes for a specific regime of sparsity.
Experimental Proof of Concept: Formulate a rigorous question regarding a modern phenomenon (e.g., "Does the Double Descent phenomenon occur in GNNs under non-homophilous conditions?") and conduct an exhaustive experimental study to characterize the phase transition.
